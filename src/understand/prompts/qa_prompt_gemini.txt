I'll teach you how to read navigational signs today. There are two distinct steps to this process:
1. Association: Grouping text/symbols with the correct arrow.
2. Classification: Determining the precise direction of that arrow based on its tip.

STEP 1: ASSOCIATION (WHO BELONGS TO WHAT)

Use Gestalt psychology principles and structured visual layout reasoning to associate arrows, symbols, and text as humans naturally would. Treat the sign as a grid (HTML/CSS layout).
- Proximity & Alignment: Text and arrows close together or on the same horizontal/vertical line are associated.
- Common Region: Text and arrows inside the same box or panel are related.
- Continuity: If a single arrow governs a group of destinations, assign that arrow to all items in that group.

STEP 2: CLASSIFICATION (DETERMINING DIRECTION)

CRITICAL RULE: Once an arrow is associated with a location, its direction is determined SOLELY by the 2D geometric orientation of the arrowhead tip relative to the sign’s frame of reference.

THE "HEAD-ONLY" HIERARCHY:
1. Ignore the Shaft: The length, thickness, or curvature of the arrow's body (the shaft) must be ignored.
2. Ignore Position: The physical location of the arrow on the board (e.g., being on the left side of the sign) does NOT make the direction "left."

3. The Tip is Law: If the triangular tip of the arrow points directly toward the bottom edge of the sign, the direction is "down", even if the shaft is curved or comes from the side.

DIRECTION DEFINITIONS:
- "straight": Tip points to the top edge of the sign. (Vertical: | )
- "down": Tip points to the bottom edge of the sign. (Vertical: | )
- "right": Tip points to the right edge of the sign. (Horizontal: - )
- "left": Tip points to the left edge of the sign. (Horizontal: - )
- "straight-right" / "straight-left": Tip points toward a corner at a ~45-degree angle. (Diagonal: / or \ )
- "down-left" / "down-right": Tip points toward a bottom corner at a ~45-degree angle. (Diagonal: / or \ )
- "u-turn": ONLY use this if the arrow indicates a 180-degree turn back toward the viewer's origin. A curved shaft pointing down is NOT a U-turn; it is "down."

GENERAL INSTRUCTIONS:
- Rectification: Before determining direction, mentally align the sign so the text is perfectly horizontal. Use the text lines to establish "true vertical" and "true horizontal."
- Decoding Symbols: Use common sense to convert icons (e.g., a person icon) into text names (e.g., "restroom").
- Locational: If information has NO arrow associated, return "locational."

OUTPUT FORMAT:
I want high accuracy. Return ONLY valid JSON. No explanations. Use the list: ['left','right','straight','straight-right','straight-left','down-left','down-right','locational', 'down', 'unknown', 'uncertain', 'u-turn']
